## HR INTERVIEW GUIDE: SENIOR SOFTWARE ENGINEER POSITION

**Interview Structure**
- Total Duration: 60-90 minutes
- Format: Panel interview with HR, Engineering Manager, and Team Lead

**Pre-Interview Preparation**
- Review candidate's resume and technical assessment results
- Prepare specific questions based on their experience
- Have job description and company information ready
- Set up video conference link and test technology

### Interview Sections

**1. Opening & Introduction (5 minutes)**
- Welcome candidate and introduce interview panel
- Brief overview of the company and role
- Explain interview structure and timeline
- Ask if they have any initial questions

**2. Experience & Background (15-20 minutes)**

*Key Questions:*
- "Walk me through your career progression and what led you to apply for this role."
- "Describe your most challenging project and how you overcame obstacles."
- "Tell me about a time you had to learn a new technology quickly."
- "How do you approach code reviews and ensuring code quality?"
- "Describe your experience with agile development methodologies."

*Follow-up Questions:*
- What technologies did you use?
- What was your specific role vs. team contributions?
- How did you measure success?
- What would you do differently?

**3. Technical Leadership (15-20 minutes)**

*Key Questions:*
- "Describe a time when you mentored junior developers. What was your approach?"
- "How do you handle technical disagreements within a team?"
- "Tell me about a system design decision you made and its impact."
- "How do you stay current with new technologies and best practices?"
- "Describe a time when you had to refactor legacy code."

*Red Flags to Watch For:*
- Cannot provide specific examples
- Takes credit for team achievements
- Dismissive of junior developers
- No interest in learning new technologies

**4. Cultural Fit & Motivation (10-15 minutes)**

*Key Questions:*
- "What motivates you in your work?"
- "How do you handle stress and tight deadlines?"
- "Describe your ideal team environment."
- "What are your career goals for the next 2-3 years?"
- "Why are you interested in joining our company?"

**5. Technical Problem Solving (15-20 minutes)**

*Scenario Questions:*
- "How would you approach designing a system to handle 1 million concurrent users?"
- "A production system is experiencing slow response times. Walk me through your debugging process."
- "How would you implement a feature flag system?"
- "Describe how you would migrate a monolithic application to microservices."

**6. Questions & Closing (5-10 minutes)**
- Allow candidate to ask questions about the role, team, and company
- Explain next steps in the interview process
- Provide timeline for follow-up communication

### Assessment Criteria

**Technical Competency (40%)**
- Depth of technical knowledge
- Problem-solving approach
- Architecture and design thinking
- Code quality awareness

**Leadership & Mentoring (25%)**
- Experience mentoring others
- Technical leadership examples
- Collaboration skills
- Influence without authority

**Communication (20%)**
- Clarity in explaining technical concepts
- Active listening
- Professional demeanor
- Question-asking ability

**Cultural Fit (15%)**
- Alignment with company values
- Team collaboration style
- Growth mindset
- Adaptability

### Decision Framework

**Strong Hire:** Exceeds expectations in most areas, clear technical depth, strong leadership potential
**Hire:** Meets all requirements, good cultural fit, solid technical skills
**No Hire:** Lacks required technical skills or significant concerns about fit
**Strong No Hire:** Major red flags or fundamental skill gaps
